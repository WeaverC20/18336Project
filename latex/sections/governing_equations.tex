\section{Governing Equations}\label{sec:governing}

\subsection{McNabb--Foster model}

The McNabb--Foster equations \cite{mcnabb1963} describe the coupled transport of mobile and trapped hydrogen in a metal lattice. The mobile concentration $c_m(x,t)$ obeys a diffusion equation with source and trapping--detrapping coupling, while each trap population $c_{t,i}(x,t)$ evolves according to local kinetics:
\begin{align}
  \frac{\partial c_m}{\partial t}
    &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\!\left(D(T)\,\frac{\partial c_m}{\partial x}\right)
       + S(x,t)
       - \sum_{i} \frac{\partial c_{t,i}}{\partial t},
  \label{eq:mobile} \\[4pt]
  \frac{\partial c_{t,i}}{\partial t}
    &= \nu_{m,i}(T)\,c_m\bigl(n_i - c_{t,i}\bigr)
       - \nu_{r,i}(T)\,c_{t,i}.
  \label{eq:trapped}
\end{align}
Here $D(T)$ is the temperature-dependent diffusivity, $S(x,t)$ is a volumetric source term (e.g., ion implantation), $n_i$ is the density of trap sites of type $i$, and $\nu_{m,i}$ and $\nu_{r,i}$ are the trapping and detrapping rates, respectively. All temperature-dependent coefficients follow Arrhenius laws:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arrhenius}
  D(T) = D_0\,e^{-E_D/k_B T}, \qquad
  \nu_{m,i}(T) = \nu_{m,i}^0\,e^{-E_{m,i}/k_B T}, \qquad
  \nu_{r,i}(T) = \nu_{r,i}^0\,e^{-E_{r,i}/k_B T},
\end{equation}
where $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant and $E_D$, $E_{m,i}$, $E_{r,i}$ are the respective activation energies. The exponential dependence on temperature is what makes the system stiff: a modest change in $T$ can shift the diffusivity by orders of magnitude, producing steep boundary layers and rapid transients that challenge both spatial and temporal discretizations.

\subsection{Boundary conditions}

Two types of boundary conditions appear in the experiments we consider. For TDS, both surfaces of the foil are held at zero mobile concentration ($c_m = 0$), representing a vacuum boundary from which hydrogen escapes freely. For GDP, the upstream surface satisfies Sieverts' law \cite{sieverts1929},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sieverts}
  c_m\big|_{x=0} = K_S(T)\,\sqrt{P(t)},
  \qquad K_S(T) = K_{S,0}\,e^{-E_{K_S}/k_B T},
\end{equation}
where $P(t)$ is the applied hydrogen partial pressure and $K_S$ is the Sieverts solubility, while the downstream surface is held at $c_m = 0$. In both cases, the trapped concentrations have no boundary conditions of their own; they are determined pointwise by the local ODE~\eqref{eq:trapped}.
