\author{Colin Weaver}

\begin{abstract}
We develop a Chebyshev pseudospectral collocation solver for the McNabb--Foster hydrogen transport equations and benchmark it against FESTIM, a FEniCS-based finite element code, on three problems: constant-temperature permeation with an analytical reference, multi-temperature gas-driven permeation where FESTIM's Newton solver diverges, and thermo-desorption spectroscopy with a Levenberg--Marquardt inverse problem. The Chebyshev solver achieves geometric spatial convergence, delivers matched-accuracy solutions with $8$--$10\times$ speedups, and maintains convergence in stiff regimes where the finite element solver fails. We find that backward Euler's first-order temporal error is the binding constraint on accuracy at moderate resolution, and that higher-order time integration is needed to unlock the full potential of spectral spatial discretization.
\end{abstract}
